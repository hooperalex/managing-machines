# SimpleBook — Editorial Backlog: Productivity & Management for the Agentic Age

**The thesis:** managing a fleet of AI agents looks a lot like leading a large, remote,
fast-moving team. So the canon isn't AI textbooks — it's the best books on **teams,
delegation, remote work, design thinking, systems thinking, and learning from failure**,
reread through one question: *how does this work when your "team" is agents?*

Each issue = one book, distilled (the 5 sections) **plus** a signature section:
**"In the Agentic World Order."** Two tracks feed the queue.

---

## Track A — Productivity & Management, Reread for Agentic Teams

### Cluster 1 · Leading Teams at Scale → Leading Agent Fleets
| Book | Author | The agentic angle |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| **High Output Management** | Andy Grove | Output = your leverage × your team's output. Agents are pure leverage — this is the operating manual. |
| **Team of Teams** | Stanley McChrystal | "Shared consciousness + empowered execution" is the org design for agent swarms. |
| **Multipliers** | Liz Wiseman | Be the leader who amplifies capability, not the bottleneck who approves every step. |
| **Turn the Ship Around!** | David Marquet | Intent-based leadership: give agents *intent*, not step-by-step orders. |
| **The Making of a Manager** | Julie Zhuo | Your first direct reports are now a fleet of agents. |
| **No Rules Rules** | Hastings & Meyer | Context, not control — how much autonomy do you hand an agent? |

### Cluster 2 · Remote & Distributed Work → Working With Agent Resources

|------|--------|-------------------|

| **A World Without Email** | Cal Newport | Design the *workflow*, not the messages — exactly the agent-orchestration problem. |
